Website Management Guide

A complete reference for updating and managing your Framer website. Follow each section in order and always publish after making changes.

① Getting Started
Step 1

Use the direct link provided by Ardent. This takes you straight to the YoshiStudio project.

Sign in with your credentials

Enter your email and password. Use "Forgot password" on the login screen if needed.

3
Locate and open YoshiStudio

After logging in you'll land on the project dashboard. Find the card labelled YoshiStudio and click it to open the editor.

Step 2

Canvas Overview

The Framer editor has three key areas. Understanding these before making any edits will save you time.

L
Left Panel

Three tabs: Pages, Layers, and Assets. Use Pages to switch between pages and Layers to find specific elements.

C
Centre Canvas

A live preview of your site. Click elements directly to select them. All breakpoints — Desktop, Tablet, Mobile — are shown side by side.

R
Right Panel

When an element is selected, its settings appear here. This is where you update text content, images, and component controls.

⚠️
Always check all three breakpoints after every edit

Your site has Desktop, Tablet, and Mobile versions. Edits on one breakpoint may need to be checked on the others. Never publish without reviewing all three.

② Homepage
Homepage · Hero

Editing the Hero Text

The hero tagline sits over the full-screen image at the top of the homepage. It is directly editable on the canvas — no CMS or right panel required.

1
Go to the Home page

In the left panel click Pages, then click Home.

2
Click once on the tagline text on the canvas

A blue selection box appears around the text layer.

🖼

[Screenshot: Hero tagline text selected on canvas with blue bounding box visible]

3
Double-click to enter edit mode

The cursor appears inside the text. You can now type directly.

4
Edit the text and format as desired

Type your updated copy. You can use standard text formatting (bold, italic) directly in the editor to match your preference.

5
Click outside to deselect, then check all breakpoints

Review the Tablet and Phone canvases to confirm the text looks right across all screen sizes.

6
Publish

Click Publish in the top-right corner.

Best Practice — Hero Text
  • Character limit: Keep the tagline under 80 characters including spaces. Longer copy may break awkwardly on mobile.
  • Line breaks: Aim for 2 lines maximum. Use a natural break point between a noun and a verb or adjective.
  • Tone: Short, declarative, brand-led. Avoid long descriptive sentences — this is a first impression, not a paragraph.
  • Formatting: Italicising one key word (as currently designed) adds visual rhythm. Use bold sparingly — it competes with the image.

🚫
Never add or delete the hero CMS item

There must always be exactly one item in Homepage Hero. Only replace the image inside it. Adding or deleting items will break the homepage hero entirely.

Best Practice — Hero Image
  • Resolution: Minimum 2400px on the longest edge. The hero is full-screen — low resolution will look pixelated on large displays.
  • Format: JPG or WebP. Avoid PNG for photographs — file sizes are unnecessarily large.
  • File size: Keep under 3MB. Compress at squoosh.app before uploading.
  • Subject position: Centre the subject slightly above the midpoint. The tagline text overlays the lower-left of the image.

🚨
Hero and portfolio slots: Image OR Video — never both

Each hero and each portfolio slot (Image 1 / Video Content 1, etc.) accepts either an image or a video — never both at once. If both fields in the same slot are populated, the page will behave unpredictably. Always leave one field empty.

🔴
The Slug field: once published, never change it

The slug is the unique URL for this service page. Search engines index it, external sites may link to it, and your analytics track it by this exact path. Changing a published slug breaks all inbound links, destroys accumulated SEO authority for that page, and causes a 404 error for anyone who has bookmarked or shared the old URL. Treat it as permanent from the moment it is first published.

Adding a Brand New Service

Adding a new service is a two-part process. First create the service in the CMS, then duplicate an existing card on the homepage canvas and bind it to the new CMS entry. Both steps are required — skipping either will result in the service not displaying correctly.

⚠️
This is an advanced task

This process involves CMS bindings and canvas layer editing. Take it step by step. Do not publish until both parts are complete and you have verified the card displays correctly on the canvas.

Part 1 — Create the service in the CMS

1
Open CMS → Services(Beta)

Click the CMS icon, then select Services(Beta) from the Collections list.

2
Click "+ New Item" in the top toolbar

A blank item opens in the right panel. Service Number will default to 0.

🖼

[Screenshot: CMS top toolbar with New Item button highlighted — blank right panel open]

3
Set a unique Service Number

Use the next available number. Currently 1–6 are in use. For a 7th service use 7. This number is the binding key — it must be unique and must not be reused.

4
Fill in all required fields

Add the Service Thumbnail, Service Name, Hero Image or Video (not both), Services (Listed), Content 1/2/3, and portfolio Images or Videos (not both per slot). Refer to the field reference table above.

5
Set the Slug

Use lowercase letters and hyphens only — no spaces (e.g. brand-strategy). Once published, this cannot be changed without SEO consequences.

6
Click "Save Draft" — do not publish yet

Complete Part 2 before publishing.

Part 2 — Add and bind the card on the homepage canvas

7
Close the CMS panel and go to the Home page canvas

Press Escape or click the back arrow. Make sure Home is selected in the Pages panel.

Under the Services Image component settings on the right panel, set Title to the new service name (e.g. Branding) and set Amount to match the CMS Service Number you assigned in Part 1 (e.g. 7).

Best Practice — About Text
  • Character limit: Keep under 160 characters. This is a statement, not a paragraph — brevity is key.
  • Structure: One sentence. Lead with who you are, follow with what you deliver, close with how you do it.
  • Tone: Confident and editorial. Avoid generic marketing language like "passionate about" or "results-driven."
  • Formatting: Do not change the text style (H1). Only edit the words. The scroll animation is attached to this layer and will break if the layer is restyled.

Homepage · (in)sights
⚙ Proprietary Component — Built by Ardent

The (in)sights Intelligence Feed

The (in)sights module is a custom-engineered content intelligence component built exclusively for YOSHI(studio) by Ardent. It maintains a live, autonomous connection to your Substack publication — surfacing your latest editorial content directly onto the homepage without any manual intervention.

The moment a new post is published on Substack, the feed detects it and renders it in real time: pulling the title, canonical link, publish date, and article excerpt automatically. Image handling is governed by a proprietary three-tier priority system designed to ensure visual consistency regardless of what Substack returns.

✅
What updates automatically — zero action required

Article title · Canonical link · Publish date · Excerpt text. These are fetched live from your Substack RSS feed every time the page loads.

⚠️
Images may require a brief manual review

RSS-sourced images can occasionally load slowly or crop inconsistently. After publishing a new Substack post, check the homepage feed and upload a manual image override if needed. This takes under two minutes.

Proprietary Image Priority System

The component resolves which image to display for each post card using the following waterfall logic:

01
Manual Override
Image uploaded directly in Framer. Always takes precedence when present.
02
RSS Image
Image pulled automatically from the Substack article feed.
03
Fallback
A curated backup image used when neither source above is available.
🚨
Images are position-based, not article-based

Image 1 always displays on the first card position, Image 2 on the second, and so on — regardless of which article occupies that slot. When a new post is published and the feed order shifts, manual image assignments must be reviewed. This is by design — always check image slots after publishing a new Substack post.

💡
Two independent components — Desktop and Mobile

The homepage runs two separate instances of the feed: one for Desktop (grid layout) and one for Mobile (stacked card layout). They share the same Substack source but have independent image slots and display settings. When updating images, both components must be updated separately.

All edits made in Framer are only visible to you in the editor until you publish. Publishing pushes everything live to your website instantly.

1
Click the blue Publish button in the top-right corner

It is always visible regardless of which page you are on.

2
Confirm if prompted

A confirmation dialog may appear. Click Publish to proceed.

3
Wait for the confirmation

The button shows "Saved" when complete. Your changes are now live.

4
Verify on your live site

Open your website in a browser tab and refresh to confirm everything looks correct.

⚠️
Framer auto-saves but does not auto-publish

Your edits are saved automatically in the editor — but they will not appear on the live website until you click Publish. Always publish at the end of every editing session.

Reference

Do Not Touch

The following elements must never be modified unless you are working directly with Ardent. Changing these can break layouts, animations, or live functionality across the entire site.

ElementWhy to leave it alone
Code Overrides Controls animations like Text Reveal. Removing or altering these breaks scroll-triggered effects on the live site.
TSX / Code files The (in)sights feed and other custom Ardent-built components live in code. Editing the code will break the components entirely.
Text styles (H1, H4-B etc.) These are global design tokens. Changing a style affects every element using it across the entire site simultaneously.
Canvas layout & spacing Do not drag, resize, or reposition elements on the canvas. Only edit text content and images through the methods described in this guide.
Breakpoints Do not add or remove breakpoints. The site is built and tested for exactly three: Desktop, Tablet, and Phone.
Navigation & Footer These are shared components used across all pages. Any change will affect every page on the site simultaneously.
Homepage Hero CMS item Exactly one item labelled "hero" must exist in the Homepage Hero collection. Do not add new items or delete the existing one.
Draft pages Pages marked DRAFT are work in progress. Do not publish or delete them.
Existing card Filter values Each service card (Image 1–6) is bound to its CMS item via a Service Number filter. Changing an existing card's filter value breaks the homepage service showcase.
🚨
If something looks broken — stop and contact Ardent

Do not attempt to fix a broken layout yourself. Close the browser tab without publishing and reach out to your Ardent contact immediately. Framer auto-saves edits, so publishing a broken state will push it live to your audience.

Best Practice, Blog Posts
  • Always include a cover image on Substack. The feed pulls the article image directly. Posts without an image will display a blank card on Desktop.
  • Keep titles under 80 characters. Longer titles wrap across multiple lines and look unbalanced in the 2-column grid layout.
  • Write a strong first paragraph. The excerpt shown on the card is pulled from the opening of the article. A compelling first sentence drives click-throughs.
  • Avoid .heic image format. The mobile component automatically skips .heic images. Use JPG or WebP for all Substack cover images.
✅
Summary, the blog page needs no maintenance

Publish on Substack. The blog page does the rest. No CMS updates, no canvas edits, no image uploads, no Framer publishing required.

The Animated Headline

The large headline in the top-right of the About page is powered by the InlineTextCycler, a proprietary Ardent-built component that cycles through a list of words inline within a sentence.

💡
Two separate components

The About page uses InlineTextCycler for Desktop and Tablet, and InlineTextCyclerMobile for Phone. Always update both when changing the headline content.

How to edit the headline

1
Go to the About page

In the left panel click Pages, then click /about.

2
Click the animated headline on the canvas to select it

The right panel will show the InlineTextCycler component controls.

3
Update the Before, Words, and After fields

These three fields together build the full headline. See the field guide below.

4
Switch to Phone breakpoint and repeat for InlineTextCyclerMobile

Select the mobile version and update the same three fields to match.

5
Check all breakpoints then Publish

Click Publish in the top-right corner.

Best Practice, About Page Text
  • Body paragraph: Keep under 180 characters. This sits directly below the animated headline and acts as a supporting statement, not a full description.
  • Location strip: Format as "BASED IN [CITY] + [CITY] / WORKING [SCOPE]". Keep it short and factual.
  • Principles paragraphs: Each paragraph should be a standalone thought, 60 to 100 words. Avoid bullet points, these are editorial statements, not lists.

🚨
Never change the Name field of any form input

Each field has a Name property in the right panel (e.g. "Requirement", "Email"). This Name is the key used by the webhook to map the submission to the correct Notion column. Changing it breaks the automation entirely. Only ever edit the Placeholder field.

🚫
Do not add or remove form fields

Adding or removing fields without updating the webhook and Notion schema will break the form submission flow. Contact Ardent if you need to change the form structure.
