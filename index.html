Website Management Guide

A complete reference for updating and managing your Framer website. Follow each section carefully and always publish after making changes.

① Getting Started
Step 1

Logging In

Your website is built and hosted on Framer. All updates are made through the Framer editor using the credentials shared with you by Ardent.

1
Open your Framer editor link

Use the direct link provided by Ardent. This takes you straight to the YoshiStudio project without navigating the Framer homepage.

2
Sign in with your credentials

Enter your email and password. Use "Forgot password" if needed.

3
Locate and open YoshiStudio

After logging in you'll land on the project dashboard. Find the card labelled YoshiStudio and click it to open the editor.

🖼

[Screenshot: Framer dashboard with the YoshiStudio project card visible and highlighted]

4
You're in

The canvas opens on the Home page by default. You're ready to edit.

🖼

[Screenshot: Full Framer canvas open on the Home page at default zoom, left panel, canvas and right panel all visible]

Step 2

Canvas Overview

The Framer editor has three key areas. Understanding these before making any edits will save you time and prevent mistakes.

L
Left Panel

Three tabs: Pages, Layers, and Assets. Use Pages to switch between pages and Layers to find and select specific elements.

C
Centre Canvas

A live preview of your site. Click elements directly to select them. All breakpoints, Desktop, Tablet, and Mobile, are shown side by side.

R
Right Panel

When an element is selected, its settings appear here. This is where you update text content, upload images, and adjust component controls.

⚠️
Always check all three breakpoints after every edit

Your site has Desktop, Tablet, and Mobile versions. Edits on one breakpoint must be reviewed on the others. Never publish without checking all three.

② Homepage
Homepage · Hero

Editing the Hero Text

The hero tagline sits over the full-screen image at the top of the homepage. It is directly editable on the canvas, no CMS or right panel required. Text formatting can be adjusted freely to match your preference.

1
Go to the Home page

In the left panel click Pages, then click Home.

2
Click once on the tagline text on the canvas

A blue selection box appears around the text layer.

🖼

[Screenshot: Hero tagline selected on canvas with blue bounding box around the text layer]

3
Double-click to enter edit mode

The cursor appears inside the text. You can now type and format directly.

4
Edit and format the text as desired

Type your updated copy. Use bold or italic formatting as preferred, the component allows free formatting.

5
Click outside to deselect, then check all breakpoints

Review Tablet and Phone canvases to confirm the text looks correct across all screen sizes.

6
Publish

Click Publish in the top-right corner.

Best Practice, Hero Text
  • Character limit: Keep under 80 characters including spaces. Longer copy breaks awkwardly on mobile.
  • Line breaks: Aim for 2 lines maximum. Break naturally between a noun and a verb or adjective.
  • Tone: Short, declarative, brand-led. Avoid long descriptive sentences, this is a first impression.
  • Formatting: Italicising one key word adds visual rhythm. Use bold sparingly, it competes with the image behind.

Replacing the Hero Image

The hero image is managed through the Framer CMS, not directly on the canvas. This ensures it stays correctly linked across all breakpoints.

1
Open the CMS panel

Click the CMS icon in the top-left toolbar (stacked cylinders icon).

🖼

[Screenshot: Top-left Framer toolbar with the CMS icon circled]

Select Homepage Hero from the Collections list

In the left sidebar of the CMS panel click Homepage Hero.

🖼

[Screenshot: CMS collections left sidebar with Homepage Hero selected and highlighted]

3
Click on the "hero" row

There is exactly one item in this collection labelled hero. Click anywhere on that row to open it on the right side.

🖼

[Screenshot: The hero row selected in the Homepage Hero collection with the right panel open showing slug and Image field]

4
Click the image thumbnail under the Image field

The image editor popup opens.

🖼

[Screenshot: Right panel with Image field visible, cursor hovering over the current image thumbnail]

5
Click the image preview inside the popup and upload your new image

Select your file from your computer. The new image replaces the current one immediately.

🖼

[Screenshot: Image popup open showing the current image preview, Resolution, Alt Text and Focal Point fields]

6
Update the Alt Text field

Write a short description of the new image (e.g. "Model in white studio, seated"). This is important for SEO and accessibility.

7
Close the popup and Publish

Click Publish in the top-right corner.

🚫
Never add or delete the hero CMS item

There must always be exactly one item in Homepage Hero. Only replace the image inside it. Adding or deleting items will break the homepage hero entirely.

Best Practice, Hero Image
  • Resolution: Minimum 2400px on the longest edge. The hero is full-screen, low resolution looks pixelated on large displays.
  • Format: JPG or WebP. Avoid PNG for photographs, file sizes are unnecessarily large.
  • File size: Keep under 3MB. Compress at squoosh.app before uploading.
  • Subject position: Centre the subject slightly above the midpoint. The tagline overlays the lower-left of the image.
Homepage · Services

The Services Showcase

Below the hero, the homepage presents a full-screen scrolling showcase of all 6 services. Each card is a full-bleed numbered panel displaying the Service Thumbnail and service name.

Every card is individually bound to one item in the Services(Beta) CMS collection via a Service Number filter. The image and title on each card are pulled automatically from the CMS, no canvas editing is required for routine service updates.

💡
Two types of task

Editing an existing service only requires updating the CMS. Adding a brand new service requires both a CMS step and a canvas step. Both are covered in full below.

🚫
Hero and portfolio slots: Image OR Video, never both

Each hero and each portfolio slot accepts either an image or a video, not both. If both fields in the same slot are populated the page will behave unpredictably. Always leave one field empty per slot.

🔴
The Slug: once published, it must never change

The slug is the permanent URL for this service page. Search engines index it, external sites link to it, and your analytics track sessions against it. Changing a published slug instantly breaks all inbound links, destroys accumulated SEO authority for that URL, and returns a 404 error to anyone who has bookmarked or shared it. Treat every slug as permanent from the moment it is first published.

Adding a Brand New Service

Adding a new service is a two-part process. First create the service in the CMS, then duplicate an existing card on the homepage canvas and bind it to the new CMS entry. Both steps are required.

⚠️
This is an advanced task

This process involves CMS bindings and canvas layer editing. Take it step by step. Do not publish until both parts are complete and the card is verified on the canvas.

Part 1, Create the service in the CMS

1
Open CMS → Services(Beta)

Click the CMS icon, then select Services(Beta) from the Collections list.

2
Click "+ New Item" in the top toolbar

A blank item opens in the right panel. Service Number defaults to 0.

🖼

[Screenshot: CMS top toolbar with New Item button highlighted, blank right panel open with empty fields]

3
Set a unique Service Number

Use the next available number. Currently 1–6 are in use. For a 7th service use 7. This number is the binding key, it must be unique and must never be reused.

4
Fill in all required fields

Add Service Thumbnail, Service Name, Hero Image or Video (not both), Services (Listed), Content 1/2/3, and portfolio Images or Videos (not both per slot). Refer to the field reference table above.

5
Set the Slug

Use lowercase letters and hyphens only, no spaces (e.g. brand-strategy). Once published this cannot be changed without serious SEO consequences, see the slug warning above.

6
Click "Save Draft", do not publish yet

Complete Part 2 first before publishing anything.

Part 2, Add and bind the card on the homepage canvas

7
Close the CMS panel and go to the Home page canvas

Press Escape or click the back arrow in the CMS panel. Confirm Home is selected in the Pages panel.

🖼

[Screenshot: Framer canvas with Home page open and Layers tab active in the left panel]

8
In the Layers panel expand: Main → Services → Card

You'll see Image 1 through Image 6, each represents one service card on the homepage.

🖼

[Screenshot: Layers panel expanded showing Main → Services → Card with Image 1 through Image 6 visible]

9
Select Image 1 and duplicate it

Click Image 1 in the Layers panel. Right-click and choose Duplicate or press ⌘D. A copy appears in the layer list.

🖼

[Screenshot: Right-click context menu on Image 1 in the Layers panel, Duplicate option highlighted in blue]

10
Reposition the duplicated card after Image 6

Drag it in the Layers panel or use arrow keys to place it after Image 6 in the stack.

🖼

[Screenshot: Layers panel showing the duplicated Image layer being dragged to sit below Image 6]

11
In the right panel set the Title and Amount fields

Under the Services Image component settings, set Title to the new service name (e.g. Branding) and set Amount to match the CMS Service Number from Part 1 (e.g. 7).

🖼

[Screenshot: Right panel showing the Services Image component with Title field set to the new service name and Amount field set to the new service number]

12
Expand the duplicated card in Layers and click the Services(Beta) layer inside it

Expand your new Image block → expand the inner collection → click the Services(Beta) layer.

🖼

[Screenshot: Layers panel expanded inside the duplicated card, Services(Beta) collection layer selected and highlighted in blue]

13
In the right panel under Content → Filters, update the Value

You'll see: Field = Service Number, Equals, Value = [a number]. Change the Value to your new service's number (e.g. 7). Leave Limit to: 1 unchanged.

[Screenshot: Right panel showing the Content section with Filters expanded, Field set to Service Number, Value field highlighted showing the updated number]

14
Verify the card shows the correct service on the canvas

The card should now display the new service thumbnail and name from the CMS. If it shows the wrong service, double-check that the Filter Value matches the CMS Service Number exactly.

🖼

[Screenshot: Homepage canvas with the new service card appearing correctly in the scrolling services section, correct thumbnail and name visible]

15
Publish

Once the card looks correct, click Publish to push everything live.

🚫
Never change the Filter value on an existing card

Cards Image 1 through Image 6 are already correctly bound to their CMS items. Only change the Filter value on newly duplicated cards. Changing an existing card's filter will break the homepage service showcase.

Best Practice, About Text
  • Character limit: Keep under 160 characters. This is a brand statement, brevity and precision are everything.
  • Structure: One sentence. Lead with who you are, follow with what you deliver, close with how you do it.
  • Tone: Confident and editorial. Avoid generic language like "passionate about" or "results-driven."
  • Formatting: Do not change the text style, only edit the words. The scroll animation is attached to this layer and will break if the layer is restyled.
🚫
Do not touch Code Overrides on this layer

The about text has a Text_Reveal scroll animation attached under Code Overrides in the right panel. Never remove or modify this, it controls the cinematic text entrance on the live site.

⚙ Proprietary Component, Built by Ardent

The (in)sights Intelligence Feed

The (in)sights module is a custom-engineered content intelligence component built exclusively for YOSHI(studio) by Ardent. It maintains a live, autonomous connection to your Substack publication, surfacing your latest editorial content directly on the homepage with zero manual intervention required.

The moment a new post is published on Substack, the feed detects the update and renders it in real time: pulling the title, canonical link, publish date, and article excerpt automatically. Image resolution is governed by a proprietary three-tier priority system engineered to ensure visual consistency regardless of what the RSS source returns.

✅
What updates automatically, zero action required

Article title · Canonical link · Publish date · Excerpt text. These are fetched live from your Substack RSS on every page load.

⚠️
Images may require a brief manual review

RSS-sourced images can occasionally load slowly or crop inconsistently depending on what Substack returns. After publishing a new post, check the homepage feed and upload a manual image override if needed, this takes under two minutes.

Proprietary Image Priority System

01
Manual Override
Image uploaded directly in Framer. Always takes precedence when present.
02
RSS Image
Image pulled automatically from the Substack article feed.
03
Fallback
A curated backup used when neither source above is available.
🚨
Images are position-based, not article-based

Image 1 always displays on the first card position, Image 2 on the second, and so on, regardless of which article occupies that slot. When a new post is published and the feed order shifts, manual image assignments must be reviewed. Always check image slots after publishing a new Substack post.

💡
Two independent components, Desktop and Mobile

The homepage runs two separate instances of the feed: one for Desktop (grid layout) and one for Mobile (stacked card layout). They share the same Substack source but have independent image slots and display settings. When updating images, both must be updated separately.

The right panel shows Image 1, Image 2, Image 3 slots alongside the other controls.

🖼

[Screenshot: SubstackFeed component selected on Desktop canvas, right panel showing Image 1, 2, 3 upload slots]

4
Check whether the images look correct

If the RSS images look fine, no action needed. If an image is missing, slow to load, or badly cropped, upload an override.

5
Click Upload next to the relevant Image slot

Image 1 = first card position, Image 2 = second, Image 3 = third. Upload a clean, well-cropped image.

Switch to the Phone breakpoint and update the Mobile component

Select the SubstackFeedMobile component and update its image slots independently.

💡

If you increase Posts beyond 3

Additional image slots (Image 4, 5, 6) appear in the panel. Check those slots too and remember to update both Desktop and Mobile components.

Publishing

Publishing Your Changes

All edits made in Framer are visible only to you in the editor until you publish. Publishing pushes everything live to your website instantly.

1
Click the blue Publish button in the top-right corner

It is always visible regardless of which page you are on.

2
Confirm if prompted

A confirmation dialog may appear. Click Publish to proceed.

3
Wait for the confirmation

The button shows "Saved" when complete. Your changes are now live.

4
Verify on your live site

Open your website in a browser tab and refresh to confirm everything looks correct.

⚠️
Framer auto-saves but does not auto-publish

Your edits are saved automatically in the editor but will not appear on the live website until you click Publish. Always publish at the end of every editing session.

Reference

Do Not Touch

The following elements must never be modified unless you are working directly with Ardent. Changing these can break layouts, animations, or live functionality across the entire site.

ElementWhy to leave it alone
Code OverridesControls animations like Text Reveal. Removing or altering these breaks scroll-triggered effects on the live site.
TSX / Code filesThe (in)sights feed and all other Ardent-built components live in code. Editing them will break the components entirely.
Text styles (H1, H4-B etc.)These are global design tokens. Changing one style affects every element using it across the entire site simultaneously.
Canvas layout and spacingDo not drag, resize, or reposition elements. Only edit text and images using the methods described in this guide.
BreakpointsDo not add or remove breakpoints. The site is built and tested for exactly three: Desktop, Tablet, and Phone.
Navigation and FooterShared components across all pages. Any change affects every page simultaneously.
Homepage Hero CMS itemExactly one item labelled "hero" must exist. Do not add new items or delete the existing one.
Draft pagesPages marked DRAFT are work in progress. Do not publish or delete them.
Existing card Filter valuesEach service card is bound to its CMS item via a Service Number filter. Changing an existing card's filter breaks the homepage showcase.
🚨
If something looks broken, stop and contact Ardent

Do not attempt to fix a broken layout yourself. Close the browser tab without publishing and contact your Ardent contact immediately. Framer auto-saves, so publishing a broken state will push it live to your audience.
